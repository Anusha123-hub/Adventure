Tennis

Tennis is a racket sport played by two players (singles) or four players (doubles) on a rectangular court with a net in the middle. The objective of the game is to hit the ball with a racket so that it bounces on the opponent's half of the court and the opponent is unable to return it.

The game is played with a felt-covered rubber ball and rackets made of various materials. The ball must be hit so that it bounces on the opponent's half of the court and then on the opponent's side of the court. If the ball hits the net and then bounces on the opponent's side, it is still considered a valid shot. Players take turns serving and returning the ball, and the rally continues until one player fails to return the ball or commits a fault

Basic Things Needed To Play

  • Tennis racket

    • A racket with a long handle and a stringed surface, used to hit the ball.

  • Tennis ball

    • A felt-covered rubber ball, used for playing the game.

  • Tennis court

    • A rectangular court with a net in the middle, used for playing the game.

  • Tennis shoes

    • Shoes designed specifically for tennis, with good ankle support and traction.
