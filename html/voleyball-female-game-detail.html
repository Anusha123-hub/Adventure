Voleyball

Volleyball is a team sport played by two teams of six players each. The objective of the game is to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's court, or by causing the opponent to make a fault.

The game is played on a court divided into two halves by a net. The court is further divided into several sections, including the attack line, the end lines, and the service line. Players advance the ball up the court by hitting it with their hands or arms, and must prevent the ball from touching the ground on their own court. If the ball touches the ground on the opponent's court, the team that hit the ball scores a point.

Basic Things Needed To Play

  • Volleyball

    • A ball specifically designed for volleyball, with a specific size and weight.

  • Volleyball net

    • A net, typically made of mesh or rope, used to divide the court into two halves.

  • Volleyball court

    • A rectangular court, divided into two halves by a net, used for playing the game.

  • Volleyball shoes

    • Shoes designed specifically for volleyball, with good ankle support and traction.

  • Volleyball knee pads

    • Protective pads worn on the knees, used to prevent injury during diving and sliding.
