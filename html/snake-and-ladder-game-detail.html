Snake and Ladder

Snake and Ladder is a classic board game of chance and strategy, played by 2-4 players. The game board consists of a grid of numbered squares, with some squares featuring snakes or ladders that connect to other squares. Players roll dice to determine how many spaces to move, aiming to reach the final square (usually numbered 100). The game is simple: roll the dice, move your token, and follow the instructions on the square you land on. If you land on a square with a ladder, you climb up to the connected square, but if you land on a square with a snake, you slide down to the connected square. The first player to reach the final square wins! The game teaches numbers, counting, and basic game rules, making it a great introduction to board games for children.

Basic Things Needed To Play

  • Game board:

    • A rectangular board with a grid of squares, usually 10x10.

  • Dice:

    • One or two six-sided dice to determine the number of spaces to move.

  • Numbered squares:

    • The game board has numbered squares from 1 to 100.

  • Snakes and ladders:

    • Some squares have snakes or ladders that connect to other squares.

  • Players:

    • 2-4 players can play the game.
