Swimming

Swimming is a water-based sport that involves propelling oneself through the water using the arms and legs. There are several different strokes used in swimming, including freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly. The objective of swimming is to swim a set distance in the shortest time possible. The distance and stroke used in a race depend on the event and level of competition.

Swimmers compete in lanes, with each swimmer starting and finishing in their own lane. The swimmer with the fastest time in their lane is declared the winner of the race. Swimming races are started by a starting signal, such as a horn or a whistle. Swimmers must remain still until the starting signal is given, and any movement before the signal is given is considered a false start and may result in disqualification.

Basic Things Needed To Play

  • Swimming pool

    • A body of water, typically rectangular in shape, used for swimming.

  • Swimming suit

    • A tight-fitting suit made of stretchy material, used for swimming..

  • Goggles

    • Protective eyewear worn to keep water out of the eyes.

  • Swim cap

    • A tight-fitting cap worn to keep hair out of the face and reduce drag.
